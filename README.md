## Methodology & Criteria

### Search Filters Applied
- **Square Footage:** 2,000–3,000 sqft
- **Bedrooms:** 3+ 
- **Bathrooms:** 2+
- **Year Built:** Pre-2020 OR Post-2021 (excluding 2020–2021 COVID-era construction)
- **Stories:** 1–2
- **Pool:** Excluded (no pool)

### Quality Builders (Target List)
Highland Homes, Toll Brothers, Drees Custom Homes, Darling Homes, Shaddock Homes, David Weekley Homes, Normandy Homes, Coventry Homes, Grand Homes, Huntington Homes, Southgate Homes

### Foundation Risk Assessment
North Texas has expansive clay soil (high plasticity index) that causes foundation movement. Areas with:
- **Lower risk:** Sandy loam soils (western Collin County, parts of Denton County)
- **Higher risk:** Heavy clay soils (eastern Dallas County, parts of Plano)
- **Mitigation:** Post-tension slab foundations, proper drainage, soaker hoses during drought

### Roof Risk Assessment
North Texas faces hail and wind risks. Premium roofing materials (Class 4 impact-resistant shingles) reduce insurance costs. Hail Alley runs through Collin and Denton counties.
