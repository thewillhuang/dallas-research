# North Dallas Suburbs: Safety, Schools, Builders & Foundation Research
> Compiled from Areavibes, NeighborhoodScout, GreatSchools, FBI UCR data, local builder reviews, and North Texas geotechnical reports.
> Data approx. 2023-2025. Crime is per 1,000 residents.

---

## 1. PLANO (Collin County)
- **Population**: ~290,000
- **Violent crime /1K**: ~1.5 (national avg 4.0; very low)
- **Property crime /1K**: ~14.2 (near national avg 19.6; low-moderate)
- **Sex offender density**: Low (~0.3 per 1K); concentrated near US-75 corridor and older east side
- **High School GreatSchools ratings**:
  - Plano West Senior High: 9/10
  - Plano East Senior High: 7/10
  - Plano Senior High: 8/10
  - Clark High: 8/10
  - Vines High: 5/10
- **Common builders**: Highland Homes, Darling Homes, First Texas Homes, Meritage, Drees, Toll Brothers (older luxury pockets)
- **Builder quality**: Highland and Darling generally well-regarded (custom/semi-custom quality). Drees mixed reviews on fit/finish. First Texas Homes has received BBB complaints about warranty responsiveness.
- **Foundation/roof**: Expansive clay soil throughout. West side (newer) generally less differential movement. East side older homes (1970s-1980s slab-on-grade) more prone to foundation cracks. Post-tension slabs in many 1990s+ builds mitigate but don't eliminate risk. Hail risk = high; roof claims common for asphalt shingle degradation after 10-15 years. Builders with better structural engineering (Highland) show fewer class-action foundation claims.

## 2. FRISCO (Collin/Denton Counties)
- **Population**: ~220,000
- **Violent crime /1K**: ~0.9 (among lowest in DFW metro)
- **Property crime /1K**: ~11.5 (below national avg)
- **Sex offender density**: Very low (~0.15 per 1K); tightly restricted in master-planned communities
- **High School GreatSchools ratings**:
  - Liberty High: 9/10
  - Frisco High: 8/10
  - Independence High: 9/10
  - Heritage High: 8/10
  - Centennial High: 8/10
  - Lone Star High: 9/10
  - Reedy High: 9/10
  - Wakeland High: 9/10
  - Memorial High: 8/10
  - Emerson High: 7/10 (newer)
- **Common builders**: Darling Homes, Highland Homes, Drees Custom, Shaddock, Huntington, David Weekley, First Texas Homes, Bloomfield, American Legend, Toll Brothers
- **Builder quality**: Shaddock and Huntington generally have good reputation; Bloomfield has had some negative reviews on material quality; David Weekley above average. Many homes in Frisco are newer (2000s+) with post-tension slabs, reducing but not eliminating foundation concerns.
- **Foundation/roof**: Frisco sits on Eagle Ford Shale and Taylor Marl -- highly expansive. Many subdivisions in far west Frisco have higher plasticity index soils. City requires engineered foundations; most have post-tension cable slabs. Hairline cosmetic cracking still common. Newer builds with moisture-maintained foundations (soaker hoses) do better. Hail risk = very high (Collin County hail alley). Roof replacements common every 8-12 years. Most builders use asphalt composition shingles; some premium builders (Toll, Darling) offer Class 4 impact-rated shingles or metal.

## 3. ALLEN (Collin County)
- **Population**: ~110,000
- **Violent crime /1K**: ~1.1 (very low)
- **Property crime /1K**: ~13.5 (below national avg)
- **Sex offender density**: Low (~0.25 per 1K)

- **Sex offender density**: Low (~0.2 per 1K)

## 5. COPPELL (Dallas/Denton Counties)
- **Population**: ~42,000
- **Violent crime /1K**: ~0.8 (extremely low)
- **Property crime /1K**: ~9.5 (lowest among target cities)
- **Sex offender density**: Very low (~0.1 per 1K)
- **High School GreatSchools ratings**:
  - Coppell High: 9/10
  - New Tech High @ Coppell: 8/10
- **Common builders**: Highland Homes, Drees Custom, Darling, David Weekley, Grand Homes
- **Builder quality**: Smaller city with fewer production builders. Grand Homes has mixed reputation -- some praise design, others report warranty disputes (multiple BBB complaints). Highland and Drees are the gold standard here.
- **Foundation/roof**: Coppell sits on Woodbine Formation -- moderate to high plasticity clay. Many homes built 1980s-1990s on conventional slabs (pre-post-tension era); these show more foundation movement. Post-2000 neighborhoods (North Coppell) have post-tension slabs and fewer issues. Hail risk = high. Older homes may need roof replacement sooner. Soaker hose usage strongly advised.

- **Property crime /1K**: ~8.2 (lowest among target cities)
- **Sex offender density**: Very low (~0.08 per 1K)

- **Common builders**: Highland Homes, Darling, Drees, Toll Brothers, Grand Homes, Shaddock

Western edges near Grapevine Lake have more bedrock and less soil movement; eastern areas have expansive clay. Post-tension slabs are standard in newer sections (Wellington, Bridlewood). Older neighborhoods (1970s-1980s) have more foundation problems. The city is known for active HOA enforcement of foundation watering requirements. Hail risk = very high (western Denton County hail corridor). Class 4 impact-rated shingles strongly recommended.

- **Population**: ~32,000

- **Property crime /1K**: ~10.0 (low)
- **Sex offender density**: Very low (~0.05 per 1K)
- **High School GreatSchools ratings**:
  - Carroll Senior High: 10/10 (multiple years; consistently top-ranked in DFW)
- **Common builders**: Toll Brothers, Darling, Highland, Drees, custom boutique builders (Hawkins-Welwood, Sharif & Munir)

Builders routinely do soil injection/stabilization for high-end builds. Foundation issues still occur but are more proactively managed. Hail risk = high (Tarrant County). Many homes have upgraded roofing (slate, tile, or Class 4 impact-resistant). Roof replacement frequency: ~15-20 years for premium materials.

- **Property crime /1K**: ~16.0 (highest among target cities; near national avg)

Older homes (1950s-1980s) frequently have foundation issues without post-tensioning. Newer builds in far north Carrollton (Hebron area) are better. Hail risk = high. Roof issues are common in older housing stock where deferred maintenance is more prevalent.

## KEY NORTH TEXAS FOUNDATION/ROOF NOTES

**Expansive Clay Soil (all cities affected)**:
- Eagle Ford Shale, Taylor Marl, Woodbine Formation -- all highly plastic (PI 30-60+)
- Soil can swell 2-4 inches between dry and wet conditions
- Seasonal differential movement is the #1 cause of residential foundation cracks
- Best mitigation: Post-tension cable slabs, proper grading, working soaker hose system around perimeter (watering foundation during drought), root barriers near trees

**Builders with Fewer Reported Foundation Issues** (based on local engineering reports and BBB complaints):
- Highland Homes: Fewest complaints per homes built; standard engineered post-tension slabs
- Darling Homes: Good structural engineering; proactive warranty
- Toll Brothers: Premium soil prep (sometimes lime injection for stabilization)
- Drees Custom: Above average

**Builders with More Reported Issues**:
- DR Horton: Higher volume = more absolute complaints; some class-action foundation lawsuits in DFW
- Lennar: Mixed -- some subdivisions have settlement complaints
- Grand Homes: Multiple BBB complaints related to foundation/structural warranty

**Roof/Hail**:
- All 10 cities lie within "Hail Alley" (Collin, Denton, Tarrant counties among highest hail claim frequency in the U.S.)
- Typical asphalt shingle roof lifespan in this area: 10-15 years (vs. 20-25 year manufacturer rating)
- Class 4 impact-rated shingles reduce insurance premiums and last longer
- Metal roofing (standing seam) is growing in popularity for longevity
- Tile/slate roofs common on high-end homes in Southlake, Colleyville, Coppell
- Insurance deductibles for wind/hail in this region are typically 1-2% of dwelling coverage
